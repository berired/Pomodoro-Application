# Product

## Register

product

## Users

College and university students managing study sessions, class schedules, and assignments. They open the app at their desk before or during a study session — daylight or lamplight, focused mindset, wanting to reduce friction and stay on task. Not a casual user; they return daily.

## Product Purpose

A unified student productivity workspace. Replaces the fragmented setup of a phone timer, a separate music app, a physical planner, and a Canvas tab. Core loop: start a Pomodoro session, put on music, check what's due, get it done.

## Brand Personality

Composed, precise, purposeful. Like a well-made notebook — nothing decorative that doesn't earn its place.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS cream: off-white + subtle shadows + endless rounded cards. Every AI-generated interface defaults to this. Avoid.
- Gamified or badge-heavy: no confetti, no XP bars, no streaks as trophies. The heatmap exists as a quiet record, not a reward system.
- Dark neon: no glowing purple/cyan on near-black, no cyberpunk or lo-fi gaming aesthetic.
- Corporate/enterprise: no navy-and-gray "productivity suite" feel.

## Design Principles

1. **The timer is the center of gravity.** Every design decision on the dashboard should support focus, not compete with it.
2. **Quiet by default, expressive when earned.** Color, animation, and visual weight are used sparingly so they carry meaning when they appear.
3. **Utility over decoration.** No element exists for atmosphere alone. Cards, borders, and icons must justify their presence.
4. **Consistent spatial rhythm.** Spacing is varied for hierarchy, not randomly — the eye should flow naturally between sections.
5. **Accessible without asterisks.** Focus states are visible, contrast is WCAG AA minimum, and interactive targets are never ambiguous.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Visible focus rings on all interactive elements. Color is never the sole carrier of information (e.g., error states use both color and text). Reduced motion: animations should respect `prefers-reduced-motion`.
